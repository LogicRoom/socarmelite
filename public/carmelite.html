About Carmelite (Pete).

The Digital Burnout.

For two decades, my entire world existed behind a screen. I built an industry leading methodology for software engineers, scaled an online coaching business to £40k a month, led multi-million dollar sales & marketing campaigns in wealth management, and even built a global portfolio of early stage mining companies in places most people have never heard of. I hit what most consider "success." The reality? It was an endless cycle of remote work, burnout, and absolute digital isolation.

Becoming Carmelite.

In 2023, I walked away. Having operated in the music scene under the alias 'Deepscale' for years; I had done well; racking up millions of plays on Youtube, but I realized how disconnected I had actually become whilst on an endless cycle of producing music completely alone in my house. I rebranded to Carmelite a slightly dark inside joke referencing an order of cloistered monks in Northern England known for their complete isolation.

The Antidote.

It was time to break the cloister. I forced myself into the London house music scene completely solo. What started as an escape turned into a massive shift: my unreleased tracks started getting support from DJ's in London, suddently I went from never doing anything with my music to having a life goal achieved and getting it played in clubs like Ministry of Sound and XOYO, and more importantly, I found a genuine, physical community.

The Studio & The Synergy.

Today, I've traded tech and finance for working on music projects from my studio in Reading. I collaborate with rising talent like Exotico; merging his raw dancefloor intuition with my 20 years of production experience. The only real hazard to our workflow is my cat, Minky, who insists on dribbling over the audio cables.

Carmelite Sessions isn't just a showcase; it’s an invitation to get off the screen, get into a great venue, and connect with people on the exact same frequency.
