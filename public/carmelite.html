About Pete.

The Digital Burnout.

For over a decade, my entire world existed behind a screen. I built a methodology for software engineers, scaled an online coaching business to £40k a month, and hit what most consider "success." The reality? It was an endless cycle of remote work, burnout, and absolute digital isolation.

Becoming Carmelite.

In 2023, I walked away. Having operated in the music scene under the alias 'Deepscale' for years, I realized how disconnected I had actually become. I rebranded to Carmelite—a slightly dark inside joke referencing an order of cloistered monks in Northern England known for their complete isolation.

The Antidote.

It was time to break the cloister. I forced myself into the London house music scene completely solo. What started as an escape turned into a massive shift: my unreleased tracks started getting played at Ministry of Sound, and more importantly, I found a genuine, physical community.

The Studio & The Synergy.

Today, I've traded tech for my studio in Reading. I collaborate with rising talent like Exotico—merging his raw dancefloor intuition with my 20 years of production experience. The only real hazard to our workflow is my cat, Minky, who insists on dribbling over the audio cables.

Carmelite Sessions isn't just a showcase; it’s an invitation to get off the screen, get into a great venue, and connect with people on the exact same frequency.
