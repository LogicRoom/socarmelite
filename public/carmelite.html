ABOUT PETE.

The Screen Time.

For years, my entire world existed behind a screen. I started out grinding as a software engineer, struggling to break through until I eventually invented a methodology to help other engineers write better code. I spun that into a consulting and online coaching business.

On paper, I had "made it"—peaking at £40k a month in revenue. But behind the scenes, it was an endless cycle of making money, hiring teams, and completely burning out. I realized that while the online world generated cash, it brought absolutely zero real human connection.

The Disconnect.

By 2023, I officially stepped away from the coaching space. I did a brief stint in finance, but it was just more of the same: remote work, more screens, and total isolation. I hit a serious low point that year. I came to the harsh realization that a life without a physical community isn't much of a life at all.

Stepping Out Solo.

In 2024, everything shifted. I had always been a house music producer in the background, so I made a definitive decision: I was going to force myself out of the house and discover the London house music scene. I started going to events completely on my own.

The Music & The Mission.

Things escalated quickly. I started getting my unreleased tracks played by DJs in iconic venues like Ministry of Sound. But the real breakthrough wasn't just hearing my music on a massive club system—it was looking around and realizing that, through the scene, I had suddenly built a massive network of real, in-person friends.

That was the catalyst for Carmelite Sessions. I decided to build something that was the exact opposite of my remote tech days. No more isolation. Just high-quality music, curated energy, and a real community built on actually getting out of the house and connecting in the physical world.
